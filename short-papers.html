Short Paper (Visnote) Track

PacificVis 2026 features a short paper track, called “Visualization Notes”. VisNotes provides an opportunity to describe significant novel works with contributions that may be narrower in scope or have limited empirical support compared to that of a full paper. The submissions can be late-breaking results or work in progress, while they should be novel enough to attract interest from the visualization community. We encourage submissions of both new material and papers not accepted by the conference paper track.

Submission Instructions

Original, unpublished short papers of up to 6 pages (reference included) are invited. Note that 6 pages is the length limit, not the expected length. All accepted papers will appear in the IEEE Digital Library. Manuscripts should be prepared according to the guide for authors given at https://tc.computer.org/vgtc/publications/conference/.
